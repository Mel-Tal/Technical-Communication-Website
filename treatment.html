    Treatment

    Fortunately, the hard-working employees here at the CDC have created an antidote that reverses the spread of the virus. Currently, due to the limited nature of the ingredients for this antidote, there are few doses available to the public. We here at the CDC are working around the clock to synthesize this antidote as quickly as possible.

    The antidote is administered by injection to a major vein and begins working immediately. In order for the antidote to be as effective as possible victims must drink plenty of water, get lots of rest, and remain as active as possible. Antidotes take approximately one week to take effect. During this week victims may still be contagious, so family and friends should remain at a distance. However, infected individuals who have received an antidote cannot contract the virus again until they are fully cured.
